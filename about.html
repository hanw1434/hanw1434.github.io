About me

I'm Han — a Computer Science student at Harvard College with a deep love for mathematics.

Background

I grew up in Vancouver, BC, where I attended Southridge School and spent much of my time immersed in competition mathematics. In 2026 I moved to Cambridge, MA to start at Harvard College, where I'm pursuing an A.B. in Computer Science (intended), Class of 2030.

Mathematics

Competition math has shaped how I think about problems. Along the way I was a winner of the 2025 Euclid Contest (top 6), placed in the second quartile of the Canadian Mathematical Olympiad in both 2025 and 2026, ranked 15th in Canada at the 2025 Asian Pacific Mathematics Olympiad, and spent the summer of 2025 at Canada/USA Mathcamp. These days I'm drawn to proof-based mathematics — abstract algebra, linear algebra, probability, and combinatorics.

Currently

In Fall 2026 I'm taking Math 55a (Honors Abstract Algebra and Linear Algebra) and exploring where mathematics and computer science meet.
