About me

I'm Han — a rising freshman at Harvard from Vancouver, Canada, with a deep love for mathematics and an unreasonable number of sports opinions.

Background

I grew up in Vancouver, BC, where I attended Southridge School and spent much of high school immersed in competition mathematics — combinatorics was always my favorite. This summer I'm attending SPARC, and in Fall 2026 I start at Harvard College, pursuing an A.B. in Computer Science (intended), Class of 2030, beginning with Math 55a (Honors Abstract Algebra and Linear Algebra).

Mathematics

Competition math shaped how I think about problems. Along the way I was a winner of the 2025 Euclid Contest (top 6), placed in the second quartile of the Canadian Mathematical Olympiad in both 2025 and 2026, ranked 15th in Canada at the 2025 Asian Pacific Mathematics Olympiad, and spent the summer of 2025 at Canada/USA Mathcamp. These days I'm drawn to proof-based mathematics — abstract algebra, linear algebra, probability, and above all combinatorics.

Sports

I play soccer, basketball, table tennis, and badminton, but I'm open to playing literally any sport. As a spectator, football (the soccer kind) comes first: I support Real Madrid and Tottenham Hotspur during the European season and my hometown Vancouver Whitecaps in MLS — and with the 2026 World Cup on, I'm cheering for Canada 🇨🇦 and France 🇫🇷. I also follow the NHL, NBA, NFL, and MLB, in descending order of frequency.

Projects & interests

The statistics of sports have always intrigued me, so I'm working on projects that combine statistical analysis, simple machine-learning models, and soccer analytics. Beyond that, my interests include shopping for clothes, card games, simple video games, working out, competitive trivia, and modern history and politics. I'm a curious person — I love going down rabbit holes and meticulously examining things and ideas.
